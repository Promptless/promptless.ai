When your docs ship in more than one language, the translated pages are usually the first to fall behind. A source page changes, the English version gets updated, and every other locale drifts until someone kicks off a large translation project months later. Promptless closes that gap by treating each locale as documentation to keep in sync: when your source-language content changes, it drafts the matching update for every locale you maintain.

<Aside type="note">
Localization is not on by default. You enable it by pointing a trigger at your source-language content and telling Promptless which locales to keep in sync. This page describes the approach teams use today rather than a single toggle in the dashboard.
</Aside>

## How it works

Most teams author in one source language — commonly English — and mirror it into the other locales they support. Promptless follows that model:

1. You designate a source language and the locales you maintain, typically one directory per language (for example, `docs/en`, `docs/es`, `docs/ja`).
2. Promptless watches your source-language content for changes.
3. When source content changes, Promptless drafts the corresponding update in each configured locale and opens it for review.

Because each update is scoped to what actually changed, you review a focused translation of the edit rather than a fresh pass over the whole locale.

## Set up locale sync

Localization runs on a [GitHub commit trigger](/docs/connect/triggers/github-commits) scoped to your source-language content. A commit trigger fires once when a change lands on your default branch, which keeps a single source edit from fanning out into a separate trigger for every locale.

<Steps>

1. **Pick your source language.** Choose the language you author in first. This is the content Promptless watches and translates from.

2. **Scope the trigger to your source-language directory.** On the [Configuration page](https://app.gopromptless.ai/configuration), add a GitHub commit trigger and use **trigger directories** to limit it to the source-language folder (for example, `docs/en`). Scoping to the source directory keeps Promptless reacting to source edits rather than to its own translated commits.

3. **Tell Promptless which locales to maintain.** Trigger directories scope which source changes start a run; the target locales are conveyed as an instruction, not a separate config field. Describe your locales and where each one lives — and whether you want a separate suggestion per locale — and record it as a durable instruction in your [agent knowledge base](/docs/get-the-most-out/agent-knowledge-base) so it applies to every localization run. For example: "`docs/en` is the source. Mirror every change into `docs/es` and `docs/ja`, one suggestion per locale, and keep product names untranslated."

4. **Review the drafted translations.** Promptless opens a suggestion with the translated updates. Review them the way you review any other suggestion, then publish.

</Steps>

<Aside type="tip">
Splitting the work into one suggestion or pull request per locale makes review easier: each native-speaker reviewer sees only their language. To enforce per-locale reviewers automatically, add a `CODEOWNERS` entry for each locale directory in your docs repository.
</Aside>

## Keep a human in the loop

Machine translation has come a long way, but a translated page still benefits from a native speaker's eye — especially for languages that are structurally distant from your source, where an early draft can read literal rather than natural. Keep review in the loop as you build trust in the output:

- **Review new locales closely at first.** Read the drafted translations for accuracy and tone before publishing, and lean on native-speaker reviewers where you have them.
- **Set a review cadence.** Once quality is dependable, many teams review each locale on a regular cadence — monthly is common — rather than scrutinizing every single change.
- **Turn on auto-merge only once you trust the output.** When your translations consistently land clean, you can let the localization trigger's suggestions publish automatically with [auto-merge](/docs/connect/triggers/github-commits#auto-merge-mode), which is set per trigger in your publishing policies. Start with review and turn it on once you're confident in the quality.

## Teach terminology and voice per locale

Translation quality depends on more than grammar. Product names, terms of art, and phrases you deliberately leave untranslated need to come out consistently every time. Promptless learns these conventions the same way it learns any other preference — through the feedback you give on suggestions.

When you correct a translation, [teach the convention with feedback](/docs/tune/teaching-conventions) and choose to remember it for future suggestions. Over time Promptless builds up the glossary and voice for each locale — which terms stay in the source language, how a feature name is rendered, and the tone each language should carry.

## Backfill translations that are already behind

If you're adopting Promptless with locales that have drifted for months or years, you don't have to wait for new edits to catch them up. Use [Deep Analysis](/docs/get-the-most-out/pay-down-docs-debt) to run a one-time backfill that translates or refreshes existing pages across your locales in a coordinated set of suggestions. Once you're current, the commit trigger keeps each locale in sync from there.

## Keep localized screenshots current too

Localization isn't only text. If your docs embed screenshots of a translated product UI, [Promptless Capture](/docs/get-the-most-out/screenshots) can recapture them against the interface in that language, so the images match the localized page instead of showing the source-language UI.

## What Promptless does and doesn't do

Promptless provides built-in AI translation: it drafts the localized content itself and keeps it in sync with your source. It does not connect to a translation management system (TMS) such as Phrase, Crowdin, or Lokalise. If your team runs a dedicated TMS and wants Promptless to hand off to it rather than translate directly, [contact us](mailto:help@gopromptless.ai) — it helps us prioritize the integration.
